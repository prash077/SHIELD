# Legitimate Bank SMS Formats

## How Real Bank SMS Looks

Legitimate bank SMS messages follow consistent patterns that distinguish them from fraud messages.

## Transaction Alerts (Legitimate)

SBI credit: "Your a/c no. XXXXXXX1234 is credited by Rs.5,000.00 on 21-Feb-26. Avl Bal Rs.25,430.50 -SBI"

SBI debit: "Your a/c no. XXXXXXX1234 is debited by Rs.2,000.00 on 21-Feb-26 at POS AMAZON. Avl Bal Rs.23,430.50 -SBI"

HDFC: "Rs 3,500.00 debited from a/c **1234 on 21-FEB-26 to VPA user@upi (UPI Ref No 402152XXXXXX). Avl bal: Rs 15,200.00"

ICICI: "Acct XX1234 debited for Rs.1,500 on 21-Feb-26; UPI/414XXXXXXX/user@okicici credited. ICICI Bank-!"

## Characteristics of Legitimate SMS

- Come from registered 6-character sender IDs (SBIBNK, HDFCBK, ICICIB)
- Contain partial account numbers (last 4 digits only, rest masked)
- Show exact transaction amounts with paise
- Include transaction date and sometimes time
- Show available balance after transaction
- Include transaction reference numbers
- Never contain clickable links
- Never ask for any action from the user
- Use your registered account details, not generic "Dear Customer"

## Characteristics of Fraud SMS

- Come from unknown or irregular sender IDs
- Address you as "Dear Customer" or "Dear User" instead of by name
- Contain clickable links (bit.ly, shortened URLs, non-bank domains)
- Ask you to take immediate action (click, verify, update, call)
- Create urgency (24 hours, immediate, urgent)
- Threaten consequences (block, suspend, legal action)
- Request sensitive information (PIN, OTP, CVV)
- Contain grammatical errors or unusual formatting
- Offer prizes, rewards, or refunds you didn't expect

## Quick Comparison

Legitimate: "Your a/c XX1234 credited Rs 5,000.00. Bal: Rs 25,430.50 -SBI"
Fraud: "Dear SBI customer, ur account will be block. Update KYC now: bit.ly/sbi-verify"

The legitimate message states facts. The fraud message demands action.
